Today I want to know which bear is best between a brown bear, The American black bear, a polar bear, and the giant panda

The Brown Bear

Let's start with the Brown bear. The lifespan of a Brown bear last until it is about 20 to 25 years of age, and they are found in parts of northern Eurasia and North America. Their height can go up to 5 feet and their mass is around 1,300 lbs, both as an adult. photo of brown bear with its cub

the diet of a brown bear consists of grass, fruit, insects, roots or bulbs of plants, they will also eat carrion which is decaying animals, small animals, and fish. Predators of brown bears are tigers, humans, lions, wolves, and even other bears. Even though they are giant, they still have amazing speed going 30 miles per hour!

The Polar Bear

Now let's move on to the Polar bear. The scientific name for a polar bear is ursus maritimus and they are only found in the Arctic Circle. Arctic Circle location

The Polar bear has somewhat smaller physical features than the Brown bear. The weight of an average polar bear is around 1,500 lbs, its height is 4.4 ft to their shoulder, and their life span will only reach lower than 25 years of age. The polar bear is seemingly fast too at around 25 miles per hour at full speed. Their appetite consists of seals, walruses, and even certain types of whales. Unlike Brown bears, Polar bears don't have any natural predetors other than other polar bears. Though the lack of any predetors, their population status is vulnerable and is still decreasing. Possibilities are humans hunting them or difficulties finding food.

The American Black Bear

Our next animal is the American black bear. The American black bear is very uncommon comparing to the others bears. Photo of the American Black Bear They weigh around 240 lbs, they are only 3 ft tall when standing on all four legs, and they can only be found in North America. Sadly the average life time for a black bear is only ten years, but they can reach to 30 years. Though being one of the smallest and quickest to die, the population status is actually doing very well. They are instead increasing. For being one of the smallest bears they will be our fastest at 30 miles per hour. Their diet is of grasses, roots, berries, insects, fish, and small mammals which is somewhat similar to the brown bear. The predators of brown bears are mainly aimed at the cubs. Either way the predators consists of coyotes, cougars, wolves, and bobcats.

The Giant Panda

The Giant Panda lives in south central China. They can weight up to 220 lbs, live up to 20 years, and can reach the height of 2 or 3 ft. Pandas will eat bamboo, grasses, wild tubers, or even birds or rodents. For not being aggressive, they do have many predators such as jackals, snow leopards, and yellow-throated martens. with having all these preadators their population is not doing the best possibly even worst than the polar bear.

The Result

In the end the best bear would be the brown bear. This is because of their abilty to deal with predators and they also have good strength. To add on, the environment of a brown bear is perfect for the food that it chooses.
